Video Games are Educational

Gaming has often been thought of as just a form of entertainment, a way to pass time with your friends. But many forms of gaming are educational opportunities waiting to be unlocked. Let's dive into a few:


Tetris is a puzzle game that requires you to place blocks of different shapes and sizes together, leaving no gaps. This game teaches eye-hand cordination, problem solving and quick thinking skills.


Legends of Zelda is a story based fantasy adventure game. The hero, Link, travels around the kingdom of Hyrule defeating enemies and collecting items to help him in his quest to save the kidnapped princess Zelda. This game teaches eye-hand coordination, strategic thinking, problem- solving skills.


Super Mario is a story based game. The Mario series of games requires cooperation among the different characters to accomplish the goal of rescuing Princess Peach. This game teaches eye-hand coordination, problem-solving and team work skills.


Pokemon is an adventure game. The object is to collect Pokemon characters, each with specific abilities, in order to defeat "The Elite Four" in a battle, thus becoming champion of the Pokemon Universe. The player must use the most effective combination of the Pokemon they collected in battles. This game teaches eye-hand coordination, strategic thinking, problem-solving and team work.




Why are these skills important?

  1. Eye-hand coordination is essential in today's high-tech world. Most of our children are required to use a keyboard as early as first or second grade.

  2. The Importance of Problem-Solving

    "Whether you're a student, a parent, a businessperson, or the president of the United States, you face problems every day that need solving."
    3. The Huffington Post, May 24, 2009, Watanabe, Ken

  3. Team work is a crucial skill in today's work place. Whether your job is a cashier or the CEO, most jobs require you to work as part of a team.

Gaming has become a resource for educating our children for the high-tech world we now live in. Our youth no longer sit idly watching television. They are engaged and learning skills that will benefit their lives.
